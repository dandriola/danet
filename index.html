Diana Andriola hails from northeastern Pennsylvania and has been intrigued by languages and cultures from a young age. After spending her early 20s traveling across and exploring the United States, she completed her BA in Deaf Studies at California State University in Northridge in 2013. She was accepted into the graduate program in linguistics at Gallaudet University in Washington, DC.

While working as a research assistant at Prof. Petitto's Brain and Language Laboratory she became fascinated by the study of the brain and learning. In 2014 she entered the PhD in Educational Neuroscience (PEN) program at Gallaudet. She currently is advised by Dr. Clifton Langdon and is the lead graduate assistant in his Language and Educational Neuroscience (LENS) Laboratory.

Her research focuses on the abstract linguistic mechanisms that support reading development, namely phonological awareness. Using functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS) neuroimaging, she studies early reading skills in deaf and hearing children.

Diana is also very active in science communication and outreach. She has served as lead coordinator of Gallaudet University's Young Scholar's Program Neuroscience Day, participated in translating science findings into American Sign Language, and is a member of Friends of Joe's Big Idea - a community of young scientists dedicated to improving science communication.

 Synergistic Activities

Content Consultant for International Baccalaureate Organization’s Bilingual and Multilingual Teaching and Learning Workshop (2017)

Lead Organizer with Dr. Clifton Langdon for Neuroscience Day during Young Scholar’s Program: Exploring the Sciences at Gallaudet University (2016)    
•  The Young Scholar’s Program is an annual summer science program for deaf and hard of hearing high school students that aims to expose students to deaf and hard of hearing scientists, graduate and doctoral students, and to provide introductory research experiences in a variety of STEM fields.

Served in various roles (Events Chair, Student Review Committee Chair, Executive Team Liaison) on the Visual Language and Visual Learning (VL2) NSF Science of Learning Center Student Leadership Team (2013 – 2017)    
• The VL2 Student Network is a national network of deaf, hard of hearing, and hearing undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral students researching various aspects of visual language and visual learning. The Student Leadership Team conducts grant proposal reviews, organizes annual student retreats, and coordinates lectures to promote learning, training, and research opportunities for student network members.



Organizer for the VL2 Research Brief Science Translation Workshop at Gallaudet University (2014)
• As a part of the Visual Language and Visual Learning NSF Science of Learning Center, student members organized a two-day research translation workshop with the goal of translating VL2 scientific research into American Sign Language videos. Because much of VL2's research concerns the deaf community, educators, and parents of deaf children, communicating research that is accessible to those stakeholders is critical in the translation between science and society. In total 10 research briefs were translated into ASL and have received more than 8,200 views on YouTube.
